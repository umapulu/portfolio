About Me

Hi! I’m Uma (Deepa) Pulugurtha — a senior at Cornell University studying Computer Science with a Mathematics minor. I’m passionate about algorithms, optimization, and building systems that are both elegant and practical.

My academic interests lie in algorithm design, data structures, and computational theory, but I also enjoy applying these ideas in software engineering and product-focused projects. I’ve built systems for motion planning, caching, and web applications — experiences that have strengthened my appreciation for efficient, intuitive design.

Beyond academics, I’m a teaching and community-focused learner. I serve on the Women in Computing at Cornell (WICC) Technical Committee, where I help mentor members and contribute to the WICC website. I’ve also tutored for several years, sharing my love for learning and helping others grow in computer science.

Outside of tech, I teach rock climbing, explore new coffee shops, and enjoy games like Mario Kart, Spikeball, and Minecraft. I’m always curious about how systems — whether human, physical, or computational — interact and evolve.
